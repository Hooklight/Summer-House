Frequently Asked Questions

Do dermal fillers hurt?

Most patients experience minimal discomfort. We apply topical numbing cream before treatment, and most fillers contain lidocaine for additional comfort. You may feel pressure during injection, but it's generally well-tolerated.

How long do fillers last?

Duration varies by product and treatment area. Lip fillers typically last 6-9 months, while cheek and jawline fillers can last 12-18 months. Radiesse can last up to 18 months or longer due to collagen stimulation.

Will I look overfilled or unnatural?

Not with our approach. We prioritize natural-looking results and can always add more at a follow-up appointment. The overfilled look comes from excessive product, which we carefully avoid.

What's the difference between Botox and fillers?

Botox relaxes muscles that cause wrinkles, while fillers add volume and structure. They're often used together for comprehensive facial rejuvenation - Botox for dynamic wrinkles, fillers for volume loss and static lines.

Is there downtime after filler treatment?

Minimal. You may have some swelling and bruising, but most people return to normal activities immediately. We recommend avoiding important events for a few days to allow any swelling to subside.

Can fillers be dissolved if I don't like the results?

Yes. Hyaluronic acid fillers can be dissolved with an enzyme called hyaluronidase. This is one of the safety benefits of HA fillers - results are reversible if needed.
