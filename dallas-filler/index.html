Frequently Asked Questions About Dermal Fillers

What is dermal filler and how does it work?
Dermal fillers are injectable gels (usually hyaluronic acid, PLLA, or calcium hydroxylapatite) that restore lost volume, smooth lines, and enhance facial features. They work by plumping the skin from within, lifting sagging areas, and smoothing wrinkles. Results are immediate and very natural-looking when done by an expert.
How much do dermal fillers cost in Dallas?
Filler pricing varies based on the type of filler, number of syringes needed, and treatment area. Most fillers cost $400-800 per syringe. Lips typically need 0.5-1 syringe, while cheeks may need 1-2 syringes. We offer transparent pricing during your free consultation with no hidden fees.
How long do dermal fillers last?
Most hyaluronic acid fillers (Juvederm, Restylane) last 6-12 months. RHA fillers typically last 9-12 months. Sculptra lasts 2+ years since it stimulates your body's own collagen. Duration depends on metabolism, filler type, and treatment area. Lips metabolize filler faster than cheeks.
Is filler safe? Are there side effects?
FDA-approved fillers are very safe when injected by a qualified professional. Most side effects are mild and temporary: slight bruising, swelling, or redness at injection sites (subsides in 24-48 hours). Serious complications are extremely rare. We use sterile techniques and premium products to ensure safety.
Can I get fillers if I'm allergic to anything?
Most dermal fillers are hyaluronic acid, which is naturally found in the body and rarely causes allergies. However, if you have sensitivities or allergies, discuss this during your consultation. We can recommend the best filler option for your skin type. A patch test can be performed if needed.
What's the difference between Juvederm and Restylane?
Both are hyaluronic acid fillers but have slightly different formulations. Juvederm tends to integrate smoothly and is great for lips. Restylane has a slightly different texture and excels for under-eye treatment. Both are excellent; the choice depends on your specific needs. Our injectors will recommend the best option for each treatment area.
What makes RHA filler different?
RHA (Resilient Hyaluronic Acid) is the newest generation filler technology. It's designed to move naturally with your facial expressions, creating a more dynamic result. It lasts slightly longer (9-12 months) and works especially well in areas where you have lots of facial movement, like around the mouth and smile lines.
What is Sculptra and when should I use it?
Sculptra is a biostimulator filler (not HA) that stimulates your body to produce more collagen over time. Results develop gradually over 4-6 weeks and last 2+ years. It's ideal for treating volume loss in the cheeks, temples, and jawline. Sculptra creates a more natural, youthful look by rebuilding lost structure.
Can I combine different filler types?
Yes! Many patients benefit from combining fillers. For example, you might use RHA for fine lines around the mouth and Restylane for under-eye hollowness. Or Juvederm for lips and Sculptra for cheeks. Our expert injectors create comprehensive facial enhancement plans using multiple products strategically.
How many syringes of filler do I need?
This depends entirely on your goals, facial structure, and treatment areas. Some patients use half a syringe for subtle lip enhancement. Others use 3-4 syringes for comprehensive cheek and temple restoration. We recommend starting conservatively‚Äîyou can always add more at a follow-up appointment.
Can I get fillers if I'm pregnant or breastfeeding?
We recommend waiting until after pregnancy and breastfeeding to get fillers. While FDA-approved fillers are believed to be safe, there isn't extensive safety data for pregnancy. It's best to be cautious during this important time. Talk to your OB/GYN if you have questions.
What should I do before my filler appointment?
Avoid blood thinners (aspirin, ibuprofen, omega-3) for 24 hours before treatment to minimize bruising. Avoid alcohol 24 hours before. Don't schedule fillers right before important events‚Äîminor swelling may occur for 24-48 hours. Arrive with clean skin, no makeup. Our team will provide complete pre-care instructions.
What should I avoid after getting fillers?
For 24-48 hours after treatment: avoid strenuous exercise, excessive heat (saunas, hot yoga), alcohol, and blood thinners. Avoid touching or massaging the injection sites. Sleep on your back if possible. You can resume normal activities immediately‚Äîno downtime. Makeup can be worn after 24 hours.
Can fillers be reversed if I don't like the results?
Yes! Hyaluronic acid fillers (Juvederm, Restylane, RHA) can be dissolved with an injection of hyaluronidase enzyme. This reverses the filler within hours. Sculptra cannot be dissolved but metabolizes naturally over time. This is one reason we recommend starting conservatively‚Äîyou can always adjust or reverse.
